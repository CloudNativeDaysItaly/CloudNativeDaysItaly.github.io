Hub-and-Spoke has become the default architecture for many GitOps based platforms. It is easy to understand, promises centralized control, and offers a single pane of glass for operating Kubernetes fleets. But at scale, this model becomes dangerous. In this talk, we explain why traditional Hub-and-Spoke GitOps architectures fail when managing hundreds or thousands of clusters. We show how an agent-based, pull-driven GitOps approach allows teams to keep the familiar model without inheriting its risks.
